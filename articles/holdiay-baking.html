Holiday Baking: Sustenance with a Smile!

Lucy Roth

Nothing brings joy and cheer to the holiday season like our favorite wintertime foods! Who doesn’t love to fill up their home with the scent of yummy snacks while getting to spend time baking with their loved ones? Whether it’s some hot cocoa to sip by the fireplace, latkes enjoyed during a game of dreidel, or a gingerbread cookie while singing carols with your family, it is no question that sweet and savory sustenance supplies scores of smiles. Here are favorites from the WSOC high school!

Ruby’s Gluten free chocolate chips cookies
Ingredients:
1/3 cup of Coconut
2 Tbsp of Almond Butter
1/2 cup of Coconut Sugar
1 Egg
2 tsp of Vanilla Extract
1-1/3 cup of Almond Meal (fine almond meal works best)
1/2 tsp of Baking Soda
1/4 tsp of Salt
3/4 cup of Chocolate Chips or Chocolate Chunks

Preparation:

1) In a large bowl, mix together the coconut oil, almond butter, coconut sugar, vanilla, and egg. Fold in the almond flour, baking soda, salt and chocolate chips.
2) Cover the dough and pop it in the fridge for about an hour.
3) Preheat your oven to 350 degrees, line a baking sheet with some parchment paper then using a small ice cream scoop, form your cookies and place them a couple of inches apart then give them a gentle press and bake them for about 9 to 10 minutes or until golden brown around the edges but still soft in the center, allow them to cool slightly before serving.

Luke’s English Toffee (3hrs, 30-35 servings)
Ingredients:
2 Sticks butter
1c sugar
2c chopped walnuts
8 1.5-oz Hershey chocolate bars

Preparation:

Grease or butter 9x13 cookie sheet; spread nuts on cookie sheet
Boil butter and sugar until caramel colored (15 mins), stirring constantly to keep from burning
Pour over nuts and spread quickly
Place hershey bars on top of candy mixture
When they melt, spread to cover as evenly as possible with a spatula.
When cold (after refrigeration) remove and break into bite sized pieces

Ms K’s Artichoke Dip :-)
Ingredients:
2 cans artichokes, drained and chopped
2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
1 cup mayonnaise (I like avocado-oil mayo!)
1 finely chopped yellow onion

Preparation:

Mix ingredients and bake in a lightly greased casserole dish for 30 mins on 400 until the surface is a little crisp and bubbly. Enjoy with corn chips, shrimp, or as a sandwich spread~
